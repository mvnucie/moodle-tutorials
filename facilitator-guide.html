Facilitator Guide: Administering Your NCOS Course

A brief guide to Moodle tools, grading, and course management.

Welcome, Facilitator! Whether you are new to Moodle or a seasoned pro, this guide will help you navigate the layout and lead your students to success.

1. Getting Started

Here is a quick tour of your virtual classroom:

🏫 The Course Layout
  • Center Column: The main learning space. Scroll down to see weekly videos, readings, and assignments.
  • Top Menu: Quick access to the Participants list and Grades.
  • Side Blocks: Look right for tools like Quickmail (email) and ILP Integration (final grades). The pop-out menu on the left can help you navigate the course more quickly.
  • The Participants Tab: Located in the top menu. The "Last access" column can help identify "at-risk" students who haven't logged in recently.
  • Personalizing "Facilitator Information":
    • Locate the Facilitator Information resource in the top "General Course Information" section.
    • Turn Edit Mode ON using the toggle in the upper right-hand corner.
    • Click the three dots (⋮) next to the link > Edit settings.
    • Add your contact details and whatever else you want to share about yourself, then Save.

2. Communication Strategy

As the facilitator, you set the tone. Use these tools to keep students engaged:

📢 Facilitator Announcements

Use for weekly overviews and critical updates. All students are automatically subscribed and receive emails.

❓ Monitoring Forums

Subscribe to the Questions & Suggestions forum. Answering publicly here reduces private emails.

✉️ Messaging (Quickmail)

Use the Quickmail block (on the right-hand menu) to email one student or the whole class directly from Moodle.

3. Managing & Grading Assignments

CLICK HERE for a detailed tutorial video on NCOS grading procedures.

How to Grade:

  1. Navigate: Click the assignment link in the weekly module.
  2. Start: Click "View all submissions" to go to a list of all submissions, or "Grade" to go to the first student.
  3. Check Turnitin: In the submission inbox, look for the Turnitin score/icon next to the student's file. Click it to view the report for plagiarism or AI use before you grade.
  4. The Grading Interface:
    • Document Preview: Highlight or annotate directly on student submission (center screen).
    • Using the Grading Rubric (Right Sidebar):
      • Feedback: Enter comments specific to that criterion.
      • Score: Enter a specific number out of the max points.
    • Overall Feedback: Use the main comment box for summary thoughts.
  5. Workflow: Check "Notify students" and click "Save and show next" to move quickly.

4. The Gradebook

Use the Grades tab (top menu) for a master view of student performance.

  • Grader Report: The spreadsheet view of all students and assignments.
  • Single View: Click the pencil icon next to any student or assignment name to focus on that particular student or bulk-grade a single assignment.
  • User report: This allows you to see all grades for an individual student in the same view they have. Helpful for troubleshooting any grade questions.

5. Official Records (ILP Integration)

The ILP Integration Block connects to the Registrar. You are responsible for submitting Final Grades only.

Step-by-Step Submission:
  1. Click "Final Grades" in the ILP block.
  2. Check the "Current Grade" column to see the calculated letter grade.
  3. Enter the Grade:
    • S (Satisfactory) for grades C+ or better.
    • U (Unsatisfactory) for grades lower than C+.
  4. Click "Submit Grades".

6. End-of-Course Survey (Week 8)

A link to the survey is located at the bottom of Week 8. Please encourage your students to complete this during the final week.

  • Why: Student feedback is vital for the continuous revision and improvement of the NCOS program.
  • Note: Remind students that responses are anonymous.

7. Weekly Facilitator Checklist

Start of Week Post "Welcome to Week X" announcement. Check video/reading links.
Mid-Week Check "Questions" forum. Monitor submissions.
End of Week Grade assignments. Remind non-submitters.
(Week 8: Remind students about Survey).
