Student Guide: Navigating Your NCOS Course

A quick overview to help you succeed in Moodle.

Welcome to your course! Whether you are new to Moodle or just need a refresher, this guide will help you navigate the course layout, access your materials, and stay connected with your facilitator and classmates.

1. General Navigation

When you enter the course, you will see a menu bar near the top of the screen. This is your primary control center:

  • Course: Takes you back to the main course homepage where all weekly topics are listed.
  • Participants: Allows you to see who else is in the class and message them if needed.
  • Grades: Click here to view your gradebook for the course.
💡 Pro Tip: If you ever get lost, look for the "Breadcrumbs" trail at the top of the page (e.g., Dashboard > My Courses > Course Number) and click the Course Number to return to the course homepage.

2. Communication & Community

At the top of the main course page, under "General Course Information," you will find three distinct forums:

📢 Facilitator Announcements

Your facilitator posts updates here. You are automatically subscribed and will receive emails.

🙌 Praises & Requests

A space to build community. Share life updates, prayer needs, or praises with your peers.

❓ Questions & Suggestions

Have a question about an assignment or tech issue? Post it here so everyone benefits from the answer.

3. Essential Course Information

Before diving into Week 1, always review the items in the General Course Information section:

  • Course Syllabus: This is your roadmap. Download and read it to understand course policies, grading scales, and the schedule.
  • Facilitator Information: Click here to learn more about your facilitator and find their contact information.

4. The Weekly Layout

Your course is divided into weekly modules specifying the activities for that week.

📺 Pre-Class Preparation: Look for materials you must engage with before attending class, such as:

  • Videos: Watch any lecture or resource videos linked in the week.
  • Readings: Complete assigned textbook chapters or articles.

📝 Assignments: Weekly tasks and homework are listed in the module.


5. Submitting Assignments

When you have a paper or project due:

  1. Click on the Assignment link (indicated by a page-with-up-arrow icon).
  2. Read the specific instructions provided.
  3. Click the "Add submission" button.
  4. Drag and drop your file into the box or browse your computer.
  5. Click "Save Changes" to upload your submission.
  6. Important: Click "Submit assignment" to finalize your submission, or it will remain in draft form.

6. Checking Your Progress

  • Course Grades: Use the Grades tab at the top of the course to see scores and feedback on specific assignments.
